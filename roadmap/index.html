Method Notes

This roadmap and the coaching modules were built for professional English coaching sessions with client-facing and business professionals.

The material is based on four main sources:

1
Real client-facing communication experience

The scenarios reflect common professional situations such as client calls, discovery conversations, value explanations, objections, follow-ups, interviews, meetings, presentations, negotiation, leadership conversations, and cross-cultural communication.

2
Common professional communication patterns

The material draws on recurring communication patterns used across sales, client service, account management, interviews, meetings, presentations, negotiation, follow-up, and cross-cultural workplace communication.

These patterns are turned into practical English coaching: what to say, how to structure it, how to adjust the tone, and how to practise it in realistic professional situations.

3
English coaching methodology

The lessons are built around skill blocks: useful language, weak-to-stronger examples, controlled practice, applied practice, realistic challenges, and live feedback.

This helps students build one communication move at a time instead of memorising long phrase lists.

4
Live personalisation

The modules provide the core material, but your real work situations can be used whenever they are useful.

This means the session can work with no preparation, while still becoming specific to your role, industry, and communication challenges.

The goal is not to follow a generic textbook course. The goal is to build the English you need for real professional situations.

Each module combines:
  • Professional communication strategy
  • Useful English phrasing
  • Live practice with feedback
